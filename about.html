The Beginning (1931)

Accra Academy was founded on July 20, 1931, in Mantse Agbonaa, James Town. It was born out of the vision of four young educators who wanted to provide affordable, high-quality secondary education to brilliant but financially disadvantaged boys.

Starting with just 19 students in a one-story building known as Ellen House, the school has grown into one of the most prestigious institutions in Africa.

The Modern Era

1961 - Relocation

Moved to the current expansive campus at Bubuashie ("Little Legon").

1981 - Golden Jubilee

Celebrated 50 years of excellence and adopted the school anthem.

2000s - ICT & Expansion

Introduction of modern computer labs, astroturf, and new science blocks.
